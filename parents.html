Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from parents about supporting children with ADHD

Create a dedicated, quiet study space, break homework into smaller tasks, use timers for work periods, and incorporate regular breaks. Consider using visual schedules and reward systems for completed tasks.

Share your child's diagnosis, specific challenges, successful strategies from home, and any accommodations that have worked. Maintain open communication and work together to create a supportive learning environment.

Practice role-playing social situations, encourage structured activities with peers, teach turn-taking and listening skills, and consider social skills groups. Provide positive reinforcement for good social interactions.

Use clear, consistent rules and consequences, implement immediate feedback, focus on positive reinforcement, and avoid harsh punishment. Consider behavior charts and reward systems for good behavior.
